How to get started — three steps
1
Create a Project in Claude — not a new chat
Open Claude at claude.ai. In the left sidebar, click New Project and name it something like My Practice — Claude Learning. A Project remembers everything you tell it across every session. A regular chat forgets when you close it. Everything from here happens inside this Project.
2
Paste the starter prompt below into your Project
Copy the prompt in the green box below and paste it as your very first message. Claude will ask about your practice, set up your context, then walk you through Lesson 1 step by step. You don't need to know anything yet — Claude explains where to click and what to type as you go.
3
Come back to the same Project for every lesson
Each session, open your Project and say: "I'm here for Lesson [X]. Let's go." Claude already knows your practice and where you're up to. After each session, leave a one-sentence note on what clicked and what didn't — the next lesson picks up from there.

How to run each session
1.Open your firm Project in Claude. Start with: "I'm here for Lesson [X]. Let's go." Claude knows your practice and picks up from where you left off — no re-explaining.
2.Spend the first 40 minutes on the lesson content and the hands-on task. Use real client work — not made-up examples. Produce the output described.
3.Last 15 minutes: ask Claude to quiz you. One concept at a time, answer in your own words. Ask Claude to correct anything you get wrong and explain why.
4.After each session, paste one sentence into the Project: what clicked, what didn't. The next lesson will start from that note.
